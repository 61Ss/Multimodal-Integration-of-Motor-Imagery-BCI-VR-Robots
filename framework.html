Project Overview

High-density EEG for motor imagery (MI) BCI is a powerful tool for neuro-rehabilitation, yet it remains difficult to deploy in clinical settings. The primary hurdles include the requirement for numerous electrodes (reducing portability), strong inter-subject variability, and unstable performance when patients experience varying levels of cognitive workload.


This project proposes a novel cognitive workload-aware, channel-efficient BCI framework. By integrating real-time workload detection, hierarchical EEG channel selection, and adaptive VR feedback, we create a robust loop that controls an assistive robot while keeping the user engaged and within an optimal training zone.

Key Challenges

Signal & Workload Challenges

  • EEG signals are inherently weak and easily contaminated by artifacts (EMG, EOG, motion).
  • Cognitive workload strongly affects Motor Imagery performance; excessive load leads to unstable control signals.
  • Need for robust, real-time workload detection to filter unreliable trials or adapt tasks instantly.

Channel Selection Challenges

  • High-density caps (100+ channels) are computationally heavy and impractical for daily clinical use.
  • Inter-subject variability makes "universal" channel sets ineffective.
  • Classical selection methods often ignore "hard" boundary trials and local electrode interactions.

Objective 1: Cognitive Load Detection

Aim & Task Setup

We aim to develop a real-time method to detect cognitive load. Using standard paradigms (like object assembly), we induce Low (0), Medium (1), and High (2) workload states. The system records EEG during baseline, task, and rest periods.

Two-Stage Classification Strategy

To handle class imbalance and complexity, we employ a hierarchical strategy. An XGBoost-based classifier first separates Low workload from the active states. A second stage then distinguishes between Medium and High workload. This approach improves overall accuracy to ~89%.

Objective 2: Density-Entropy Channel Selection

Motivation: From 118 to 20 Channels

High-density EEG provides rich data but is burdensome. We need a compact subset (e.g., 20 channels) that preserves decoding accuracy. Our method, CHDECS, leverages multi-view collaborative information gain and density-entropy metrics.

Core Concept

We prioritize "hard" trialsâ€”those with high entropy near decision boundaries. By focusing the model on these difficult samples and optimizing across task, subject, and group levels, we identify the most informative electrodes for Motor Imagery.

Experiments & Results

Cognitive Workload Detection

Tested on 8 subjects performing tasks at 3 difficulty levels.

  • Accuracy: Two-stage strategy improved accuracy from ~82% to ~89%.
  • F1 Score: Detection of critical Medium/High states improved by ~12%.
  • Cluster Analysis: t-SNE shows clear separation between workload classes.

Channel Selection Effectiveness

Validated on BCI competition datasets (118 channels, 100Hz).

  • Efficiency: Reducing from 118 to 30 channels retained ~99% of relative performance.
  • Portability: A 10-20 channel setup is feasible for rapid clinical deployment.
  • Robustness: The method outperforms random selection and standard Fisher score methods.
